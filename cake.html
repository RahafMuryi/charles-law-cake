Charles' Law

Baking a Cake 🎂

When temperature increases, gas bubbles in the batter expand — pushing the cake upward and making it fluffy.

V₁/T₁ = V₂/T₂
T↑  =  V↑
Step 1 — Cold batter The gas bubbles inside the batter are small because the temperature is low. The cake is flat and dense — no rising yet!
"When temperature increases, gas volume increases because gas particles move faster and take up more space."
Temperature ↑ → Volume ↑
Pressure stays constant
