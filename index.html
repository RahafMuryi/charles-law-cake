Boyle's Law — Pressure & Volume
Boyle's Law
🫁

Lungs & Human Breathing

When we inhale, lung volume increases and pressure drops so air flows in. When we exhale, volume decreases and pressure rises so air flows out.

P₁V₁ = P₂V₂  ·  V↑ = P↓

Boyle's Law

Hairspray Cans

Gas inside the can is kept under high pressure. Pressing the nozzle releases it into a larger volume, dropping pressure and spraying the product out.

P₁V₁ = P₂V₂  ·  V↓ = P↑

Conclusion

Charles' Law shows that gas volume increases when temperature increases (at constant pressure), while Boyle's Law shows that gas pressure increases when volume decreases (at constant temperature). Through these four examples — turkey timers, baking cakes, hairspray cans, and breathing — we can see that gas laws explain many real-life situations around us every day.
