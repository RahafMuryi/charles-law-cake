Boyle's Law

Lungs & Human Breathing 🫁

When lung volume changes, pressure changes — air flows in when pressure drops and out when pressure rises.

P₁V₁ = P₂V₂
V↑ = P↓  ·  V↓ = P↑
Step 1 — Normal lungs The lungs are at a normal resting volume. Pressure inside equals pressure outside. Everything is balanced — no air is moving.
"When lung volume increases, pressure decreases because air particles have more space to move."
Volume ↑ → Pressure ↓
Temperature stays constant
